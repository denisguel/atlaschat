# 1. Purpose

This document defines the long-term evolution strategy of ATLAS.

It describes how the platform grows from an AI-assisted property management system into a fully autonomous Hospitality Operating System.

The roadmap is organized around capabilities rather than release dates, allowing continuous evolution while preserving architectural stability.

---

# 2. Product Vision

ATLAS evolves through progressive intelligence.

Each stage increases:

- automation
- prediction
- personalization
- business intelligence
- owner leverage

The final objective is an autonomous AI-native operating system for hospitality.

---

# 3. Evolution Principles

The roadmap follows these principles:

- architecture before features
- intelligence before automation
- modular expansion
- backward compatibility
- continuous learning
- measurable business value

Every new capability must reinforce the platform, not complicate it.

---

# 4. Stage 1 — AI-Assisted Operations (MVP)

Primary capabilities:

- reservation management
- guest communication
- pricing recommendations
- direct booking website
- operational workflows
- basic marketing automation

Human remains in control.

# 21. Strategic Constraints

The roadmap must never compromise:

- modular architecture
- deterministic execution
- explainable AI
- tenant isolation
- security
- business ownership of decisions

Short-term speed must not create long-term technical debt.

# 22. Future Vision

- understanding every property

---

# 23. Summary

The Product Roadmap defines the strategic evolution of ATLAS from an MVP into a global AI-native Hospitality Operating System.

By progressively increasing intelligence, automation and institutional knowledge while preserving architectural integrity, ATLAS is positioned to become a long-term platform rather than a feature-driven application.
